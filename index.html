Executive Summary

ESE is an AI-powered engineering copilot for petroleum process engineers, built by EPROM. It needs a business model that reflects Egypt's economic reality — EGP devaluation, tight refinery budgets, and procurement constraints.

Two tracks, one goal. Model A sells directly to individual engineers at consumer prices — building cash flow, user feedback, and a community of practice. Model B sells to EGPC refineries at prices they can actually approve — proving the product inside real operating plants. Together they lay the foundation for high-ticket Model C/D deals from Year 4 onward.

Honest assessment: Model B is a strategic market-entry investment, not a standalone profit center. It is cash-negative through Year 3. The real return comes in Year 4+ when 10 EGPC refinery references enable Model C (on-prem appliance at 5–18M EGP per deal) and Model D (consulting). Think of Model B as the cost of building an unassailable reference base.

How the Two Tracks Fit Together

Model A → Model B

User feedback from 1,500+ engineers tells us what refiners need. Case studies arm EGPC pitches. Cash flow offsets shared costs.

Model B → Model C

10 EGPC refinery references = the credential to sell Model C on-prem appliances at 5–18M EGP per deal. Relationships compound.

Egyptian Data Sovereignty

Data stays in Egypt. AI built by Egyptian engineers. Priced for Egyptian budgets. Law 151/2020 compliant by design.

Stage Gates

① 3 pilots signed Y1Q3. ② v2 product ready Y1Q3. ③ Model A running in parallel. If gates missed → revert to Model A-only.
