How ESE Makes Money

Two simple tracks. One sells to people. One sells to companies. Together they build the case for bigger deals later.

Track A — Sell to Engineers

Individual petroleum engineers buy a subscription, like Netflix or ChatGPT. They get an AI assistant that helps them do their job faster — calculate heaters, pumps, flares, and more.

Price: 600 to 48,000 EGP per year depending on tier

Target: 2,400 paying users by Year 3

Why: Generates cash and proves engineers actually want this

Track B — Sell to Refineries

EGPC subsidiary refineries buy a company license for their engineering teams. They get the same AI tools plus their own manuals and procedures loaded in.

Price: 100,000 or 200,000 EGP per year per refinery

Target: 10 EGPC refineries by Year 3

Why: Builds the reference list needed for high-ticket deals later

The real payoff is Year 4 and beyond. Once 10 EGPC refineries use ESE, EPROM can sell bigger on-premise systems (Model C) at 5–18 million EGP each. The first 3 years are about building the proof.

Where the Money Goes

Over 3 years, the combined investment across both tracks totals 20.5M EGP. Here is where every pound goes.

Hardware & Equipment — One-time purchase of the GPU server (4× NVIDIA A100 80GB, Dell R760xa). Egypt landed price with customs. The AI brain of ESE — runs in EPROM's Cairo data center.

People (Team) — Engineers, sales, marketing, DevOps, support. Salaries for the people building and selling ESE.

Marketing — LinkedIn ads, conference booths, YouTube content, magazine ads, university programs.

Infrastructure & Hosting — Colocation (electricity, cooling, rack space) for the GPU server, plus cloud servers (AWS EC2) for the portal.

Legal & Certifications — EULA, privacy policy, trademarks, ITIDA registration.

Other — Payment gateway fees, support tools, travel, contingency buffer.

Infrastructure & Equipment — What We Run On

ESE needs AI computing power — in-house, inside Egypt. EGPC refineries require data sovereignty. No refinery data leaves the country. The strategy is self-host from Day 1 — purchase the GPU server upfront, run it in EPROM's Cairo data center. This is a capital investment: one-time purchase, then only electricity + maintenance.

In-house AI is an EGPC requirement. Elyamani's conversation with EGPC confirmed: they will adopt ESE only if the AI runs on Egyptian hardware. Cloud APIs (Claude, RunPod) are not acceptable for refinery data. The hardware purchase is not optional — it is the entry ticket to the EGPC market.
